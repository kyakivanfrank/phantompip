# Phantompip Overview

Phantompip looks like a premium AI trading platform for MetaTrader 5 users. The site is set up to onboard traders, connect an MT5 account, manage automated bots, monitor performance, and handle subscription and support flows in one place.

## What the system appears to do

The public site presents the product as an automated trading service that connects to an MT5 account and runs strategies on the user’s behalf. After signup and login, the dashboard acts like the main control center for the account, with separate areas for bots, MT5 connection status, subscriptions, settings, analytics, and a trading terminal.

## Main user flow

1. A visitor lands on the homepage and is encouraged to sign up or log in.
2. The user creates an account and moves into the protected dashboard.
3. The dashboard shows whether an MT5 account is connected and what the current plan is.
4. The user can review or manage trading bots, check analytics, inspect the terminal, and adjust account settings.
5. Subscription and support pages handle payment, activation, and help requests.

## How the product is structured

- The landing page sells the platform and explains the value proposition.
- Authentication pages handle account access.
- The dashboard is the operational area for connected accounts and bot management.
- The terminal and analytics pages focus on live trading activity and performance tracking.
- Subscription and support pages handle activation, renewal, and customer help.

## Overall read

This is a trading-operations web app, not just a marketing site. It is meant to help a user connect capital to an MT5 account, choose or tune automated strategies, watch results, and manage the service around that workflow.

## Development direction

If this is going to grow, the next useful work would be real account integration, real bot state, real payment handling, and persistent analytics data instead of the current UI-only mock state.
